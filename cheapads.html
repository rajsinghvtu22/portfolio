Problem

Outdoor advertising lacks credible viewer analytics.

Billboard campaigns are usually sold on estimated impressions, location quality, and campaign duration. Advertisers struggle to know how many people actually viewed an ad, how traffic changed over time, and whether an ad ran for the promised duration.

Solution

Computer vision telemetry for physical ad inventory.

CheapAds processes live camera frames, detects viewers with YOLOv5, counts validated detections, tracks ad runtime, and sends campaign summaries through a dashboard and WhatsApp reporting flow.

Challenges

Engineering constraints solved during development.

Lighting variation

Outdoor feeds change drastically across day, evening, rain, and shadow conditions, requiring threshold tuning and robust preprocessing.

Counting stability

Raw detections can double-count people across adjacent frames, so counting needed temporal windows and confidence filtering.

Latency budget

The pipeline had to balance frame rate, accuracy, and reporting cadence to preserve near real-time performance.

Lessons Learned

  • Production vision is more than detection. The value comes from reliable aggregation, thresholds, and business-facing metrics.
  • Model speed matters early. FPS targets influence preprocessing, image size, confidence thresholds, and deployment hardware.
  • Reports create adoption. WhatsApp summaries turn raw analytics into a workflow advertisers can actually use.
