Problem

Career guidance is often generic and hard to act on.

Learners need advice that adapts to their background, target role, timeline, and current gaps. Generic lists of courses or interview questions rarely become a usable plan.

Solution

A prompt-engineered LLM advisor with structured outputs.

The assistant collects user goals, transforms them into a normalized context, uses Gemma through Ollama, and returns role-specific career guidance, interview plans, and learning recommendations.

Challenges

Reducing generic answers

Prompt templates needed role, skill, and timeline constraints to avoid vague advice.

Reliable structure

Outputs were shaped into repeatable sections so the UI could show plans cleanly.

Local inference tradeoffs

Running through Ollama improved privacy while requiring careful prompt size and response control.
